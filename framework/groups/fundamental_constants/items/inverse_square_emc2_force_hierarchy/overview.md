# Mass-Energy Equivalence

## The Most Famous Equation

E = mc^2. Everyone knows it. Most people can quote it. But almost no one can answer the question it raises: why c^2? (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

Not why there is a conversion factor between mass and energy. That much follows from special relativity. The question is more specific. Why is the conversion factor the speed of light, squared? Why not some other constant? Why squared, rather than cubed, or to the first power? Einstein derivation shows that c^2 is consistent with the structure of spacetime. It does not explain why the speed of light is the quantity that appears, or why it appears as a square. In textbook treatments, c^2 is a consequence of the Lorentz transformation, correct but unexplained at a deeper level. (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

The stellated octahedron explains it. The answer comes from the bimetric structure of the gravitational field, and it answers not just why c^2, but four questions simultaneously. (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

## What Is Mass?

Mass is not a substance. It is an energy cost.

The gravitational field must close at every point. The metric must remain nondegenerate, and the vierbein must span the full tangent space. This closure does not happen by magic. It propagates at the speed of light $c$, the hyperbolic closure speed of Einstein field equations in tetrad formulation. The field tile has a characteristic size $r$, so the total closure bandwidth, how fast the field cycles to maintain consistency, is $c/r$. (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

But the stellated octahedron does not have a single interaction mode. It has $N$ independent states, one for each way the dual tetrahedral structure can couple internally. Closure must service each state in turn, dividing the total bandwidth equally. The per state closure rate is $c/(Nr)$. (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

One cycle at angular frequency $\omega$ carries energy $\hbar\omega$. The tile rest energy, its mass, is the energy required to complete one closure sub cycle:

$$
mc^2 = \hbar \times \frac{c}{Nr}, \quad \text{which gives} \quad m = \frac{\hbar}{Nrc} (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)
$$

Mass is the energy cost of maintaining field closure across $N$ interaction states at speed $c$. A larger tile needs less energy per cycle. More interaction states divide the bandwidth into finer slices. When the state count $N$ is identified as $1/\alpha$, derived from stellated octahedron geometry, this becomes (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

$$
m = \frac{\alpha \hbar}{rc}
$$

This is the classical electron radius relation, arrived at not from electromagnetism but from topology of field closure. (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

## Where Is Mass?

At the Field Origin. This is the single point where the two tetrahedral chambers meet, where the propagation time $\tau = 0$, and where both metric fields are evaluated simultaneously. Energy bound at this point is shared between both sectors, with each chamber boundary conditions preventing the energy from propagating outward through either one alone. A particle with mass is a stable configuration of energy localized at the coupling point of two tetrahedra. It is, in the deepest sense, light that cannot leave. (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

## Why c?

Because each tetrahedral field chamber maintains closure at the speed of light. This is a consequence of the hyperbolic structure of Einstein equations. LIGO confirmed it: gravitational waves travel at $c$ to a precision of one part in $10^{15}$. The closure speed of TetA is $c$. The closure speed of TetB is $c$. There is no other speed in the system. (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

## Why Squared?

This is where the bimetric structure speaks. Three independent constraints all select $c^2$:

**Dimensional.** Energy has dimensions $ML^2T^{-2}$. Mass has dimensions $M$. The conversion factor must therefore be $L^2T^{-2}$, a squared velocity. Adding two speeds gives $mc + mc = 2mc$, which has dimensions of momentum. Only a product of two speeds has the right dimensions. (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

**Structural.** The metric is bilinear in the tetrad:

$$
g_{\mu\nu} = \eta_{ab} e^a_{\mu} e^b_{\nu}
$$

It is a product of two frame fields, not a sum. The time time component $g_{00} = -c^2$ is the product of two frame field components by definition of tetrad formalism. The square is built into geometry of how the two tetrahedra define spacetime. (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

**Symmetric.** The two chambers of the stellated octahedron are structurally identical. The TetA <-> TetB exchange symmetry is exact at the Field Origin where $s = 0$. Among the three candidates with correct dimensions ($c_A^2$, $c_B^2$, $c_A \times c_B$), only the product $c_A \times c_B$ respects this exchange symmetry. Since both closure speeds equal $c$, the product is $c \times c = c^2$. (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

Three constraints, dimensional, structural, and symmetric, all point to the same answer. The conversion factor is not $c$ raised to some power. It is the product of two closure speeds, one from each tetrahedron:

$$
E = m \times c_{\mathrm{TetA}} \times c_{\mathrm{TetB}} = m \times c \times c = mc^2 (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)
$$

Einstein equation is not separate from the coupling hierarchy of the stellated octahedron. It is a direct reading of the dual tetrahedral field structure. (CODATA Task Group, 2022; Planck Collaboration, 2020)

Mass is closure energy bound at the Field Origin. Conversion releases that energy through two metric sectors. The factor $c^2$ is the unique exchange symmetric product of two closure speeds.
